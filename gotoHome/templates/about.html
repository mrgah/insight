A recent Harvard study estimates that more than 7 million renter households include someone with a disability, but even after the passage of the Americans with Disabilities Act, it can be a challenge for people with mobility problems to find accessible housing.

Though the Department of Housing and Urban Development periodically compiles inventories of accessible housing, these resources are out of date and have serious limitations.

For example, single-family homes make up about 40% of the rental stock in the US, but HUD's inventory only covers multifamily dwellings. Even worse, HUD lists no accessible units at all for the vast majority of ZIP codes in the US.

Access Granted attempts to ameliorate this situation by suggesting dwellings that may be accessible, drawing upon expert knowledge, housing data, and modern machine learning and artificial intelligence algorithms. Get in touch with us at access.granted.app@gmail.com if you have any feedback or suggestions!
